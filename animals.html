Warm Water Species

Warm Waters

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

Critically endangered due to habitat loss, pollution, and illegal trade of their beautiful shells. They play a crucial role in maintaining healthy coral reefs.

Learn More
Warm Waters

Whale Shark

The largest fish in the sea is endangered due to bycatch, vessel strikes, and hunting. These gentle giants filter feed on plankton and small fish.

Learn More
Warm Waters

Staghorn Coral

This reef-building coral has declined by over 80% due to climate change, disease, and pollution. Coral reefs support 25% of all marine life.

Learn More
Warm Waters

Bluefin Tuna

Overfishing has brought this magnificent predator to the brink of extinction. They can reach speeds of 40 mph and cross entire oceans.

Learn More
Warm Waters

Dugong

These gentle "sea cows" are vulnerable due to habitat loss and hunting. They're closely related to manatees and play a key role in marine ecosystems.

Learn More

Temperate Water Species

Temperate Waters

Vaquita

The world's most endangered marine mammal with fewer than 10 individuals remaining. Threatened by illegal fishing operations in the Gulf of California.

Learn More
Temperate Waters

Southern Sea Otter

Endangered due to oil spills, fishing gear entanglement, and habitat loss. These keystone species help maintain kelp forest ecosystems.

Learn More
Temperate Waters

Blue Whale

The largest animal ever known to have existed, endangered due to historical whaling and ship strikes. Populations are slowly recovering.

Learn More
Temperate Waters

North Atlantic Right Whale

Critically endangered with only about 350 remaining. Threatened by ship strikes and fishing gear entanglements.

Learn More

Cold Water Species

Cold Waters

Polar Bear

Vulnerable due to melting Arctic sea ice from climate change. These marine mammals rely on sea ice to hunt seals and travel.

Learn More
Cold Waters

Narwhal

Near threatened due to climate change and hunting. Known as the "unicorns of the sea" for their long spiral tusks, they're sensitive to noise pollution.

Learn More
Cold Waters

Beluga Whale

Some populations are endangered due to pollution, habitat degradation, and climate change. Known for their distinctive white color and vocalizations.

Learn More
Cold Waters

Antarctic Krill

Declining due to climate change and commercial fishing. These tiny creatures form the base of the Antarctic food web, supporting whales, penguins, and seals.
